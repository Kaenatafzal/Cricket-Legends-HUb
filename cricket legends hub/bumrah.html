Jasprit Bumrah made his debut for Gujarat in the 2013-14 season of the Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy, India's premier domestic T20 competition. His performances quickly caught the attention of selectors, earning him a spot in the Mumbai Indians squad for the Indian Premier League (IPL).

Bumrah's international breakthrough came in January 2016 when he made his debut for the Indian national team in a T20I against Australia.

His unique action and ability to bowl at varying speeds made an immediate impact, earning him a permanent spot in the limited-overs squads.


Bumrah made a seamless transition to Test cricket. He quickly became the linchpin of the Indian pace attack, playing a crucial role in India's historic Test series victories in Australia in 2018-19 and at home against England.

Jasprit Bumrah's journey from a talented youngster in Ahmedabad to a world-class fast bowler is a testament to his skill, hard work, and determination. As one of the leading pace bowlers in world cricket, Bumrah continues to be a key player for India in all formats of the game.
